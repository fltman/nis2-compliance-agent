# NIS2 Directive - Articles 20, 21, 22: Governance & Risk Management

## Article 20: Governance

### Article 20(1): Management Body Responsibility
Management bodies of essential and important entities must:
- **Approve** cybersecurity risk-management measures (Article 21)
- **Oversee** implementation
- Can be **held liable** for infringements

### Article 20(2): Training Requirements

**Management Body Training** - Members **must receive training** to:
- Identify risks
- Assess cybersecurity risk-management practices
- Understand impact on entity services

**Employee Training** - Entities **should offer similar training** to employees regularly

## Article 21: Cybersecurity Risk-Management Measures

### THE CORE OBLIGATION

## Article 21(1): Requirement

Essential and important entities must take **appropriate and proportionate** measures:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Technical** | Technology-based controls |
| **Operational** | Process and procedure controls |
| **Organisational** | Governance and management controls |

### Goals
1. Manage risks to network and information systems
2. **Prevent or minimise** incident impact on service recipients and other services

### Proportionality Factors
Measures must consider:
- **State-of-the-art** technology
- Relevant European/international **standards**
- **Cost** of implementation
- Entity's **risk exposure** and **size**
- **Likelihood** and **severity** of incidents

---

## Article 21(2): THE 10 MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

Based on **all-hazards approach** protecting systems AND physical environment.

| # | Measure | Description |
|---|---------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Risk analysis & security policies | Policies on risk analysis and information system security |
| **(b)** | Incident handling | Procedures for incident detection, response, recovery |
| **(c)** | Business continuity | Backup management, disaster recovery, crisis management |
| **(d)** | Supply chain security | Security of supplier/service provider relationships |
| **(e)** | Secure development | Security in acquisition, development, maintenance; vulnerability handling |
| **(f)** | Effectiveness assessment | Policies to assess if measures work |
| **(g)** | Cyber hygiene & training | Basic practices and cybersecurity training |
| **(h)** | Cryptography | Policies on cryptography and encryption use |
| **(i)** | HR & access control | Human resources security, access control, asset management |
| **(j)** | MFA & secure communications | Multi-factor authentication; secured voice/video/text; emergency communications |

---

## Article 21(3): Supply Chain Security Requirements

When assessing supply chain measures, entities must consider:
- **Vulnerabilities** specific to each direct supplier/service provider
- **Overall quality** of supplier products
- Supplier **cybersecurity practices** including secure development
- Results of **coordinated EU supply chain risk assessments** (Article 22)

## Article 21(4): Non-Compliance Correction
If non-compliance found → Must take **corrective measures without undue delay**

## Article 21(5): Implementing Acts

**Mandatory by 17 October 2024** for:
- DNS service providers, TLD registries
- Cloud computing, Data centres, CDN providers
- MSPs, MSSPs
- Online marketplaces, Search engines, Social networks
- Trust service providers

---

## Article 22: EU-Level Coordinated Supply Chain Risk Assessments

### Article 22(1): Authority
**Cooperation Group** (with Commission and ENISA) **may** carry out coordinated risk assessments of critical ICT supply chains.

### Article 22(2): Identification
Commission identifies critical ICT services/systems/products for coordinated assessment.
