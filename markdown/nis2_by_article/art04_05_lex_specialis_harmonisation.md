# NIS2 Directive - Articles 4 & 5: Lex Specialis & Minimum Harmonisation

## Article 4: Sector-Specific Union Legal Acts (Lex Specialis)

### The "DORA Principle"
**Key rule**: If another EU law already requires equivalent cybersecurity measures, NIS2 does NOT apply to those entities.

### Article 4(1): When NIS2 Steps Aside

If a sector-specific EU law requires:
- Cybersecurity risk-management measures, AND
- Incident notification

And these are **at least equivalent** to NIS2 → NIS2 provisions (including supervision) do **NOT apply**.

**Important**: If the sector law doesn't cover ALL entities in a sector, NIS2 still applies to the uncovered ones.

### Article 4(2): What is "Equivalent"?

| NIS2 Requirement | Sector Law Must Have |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Risk management (Art. 21) | At least equivalent measures |
| Incident reporting (Art. 23) | Immediate/automatic access for CSIRTs + equivalent notification rules |

### Article 4(3): Commission Guidelines
- **Deadline**: 17 July 2023 (guidelines issued)
- Regular review
- Input from Cooperation Group and ENISA

**Practical example**: DORA (Regulation 2022/2554) for financial sector entities

---

## Article 5: Minimum Harmonisation

Member States **MAY adopt stricter rules** than NIS2, provided they:
- Ensure higher cybersecurity
- Are consistent with EU law

**This means**: NIS2 is a floor, not a ceiling.
