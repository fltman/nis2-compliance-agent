# Commission Guidelines on Article 4(1) and (2) of NIS2 Directive

**Document**: C(2023) 6068 final
**Date**: Brussels, 13 September 2023
**Issuer**: European Commission

## Purpose
Clarifies the application of Article 4(1) and (2) of Directive (EU) 2022/2555 (NIS2 Directive).

## Topic
**Lex Specialis** - The relationship between NIS2 and sector-specific Union legal acts addressing:
- Cybersecurity risk-management measures
- Incident reporting requirements

## Legal Basis
Article 4(3) of NIS2 required the Commission to provide these guidelines by **17 July 2023**.
# Article 4 Guidelines - Introduction & Equivalence Principle

## I. Introduction

### Purpose of Guidelines
Clarifies relationship between NIS2 and **sector-specific Union legal acts** addressing:
- Cybersecurity risk-management measures
- Incident reporting requirements

### Appendix
Contains list of sector-specific acts falling within Article 4 scope.
**Note**: Acts not listed may still fall within scope.

### Consultation
Commission consulted:
- NIS Cooperation Group

## II. Equivalence of Cybersecurity Requirements

### Article 4(1) Key Rule: Lex Specialis

**When sector-specific acts apply instead of NIS2**:
- Entity must be essential or important under NIS2
- Sector-specific act requires cybersecurity risk-management measures OR incident notification
- Those requirements are **at least equivalent in effect** to NIS2 obligations

**Result**: NIS2 provisions (including Chapter VII supervision/enforcement) **do not apply** to such entities.

**Partial coverage**: If sector-specific act doesn't cover all entities in a sector, NIS2 continues to apply to uncovered entities.

---

## II.1. Cybersecurity Risk-Management Requirements

### Equivalence Test (Article 4(2)(a))
Sector-specific requirements are equivalent if they are **at least equivalent in effect** to Article 21(1) and (2) of NIS2.

**Minimum standard**: Requirements must **correspond to or go beyond** NIS2 provisions (may be more granular/detailed).
# Article 4 Guidelines - Risk Management Equivalence Criteria

## Article 21(1) Requirements

Sector-specific acts must require entities to take:
- **Appropriate and proportionate** measures
- **Technical, operational, and organisational** measures
- To manage risks to **network and information systems** security
- For operations AND service provision
- Measures must be **risk-based**
- Must **prevent or minimise** incident impact

**Key point**: Requirements must cover **ALL operations and services** of the entity, not just specific IT assets or critical services.

---

## Security of Network and Information Systems

### Four Protection Goals
Sector-specific measures must protect:
1. **Availability**
2. **Authenticity**
3. **Integrity**
4. **Confidentiality**

...of stored, transmitted, or processed data AND services offered.

### Network and Information Systems Scope
Must cover:
- Electronic communication networks
- Any device/group of interconnected devices processing digital data
- Digital data stored/processed/transmitted
- **Hardware, firmware, and software** used by entity

---

## All-Hazard Approach

Sector-specific measures must protect against **any type of event**:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Physical threats | Sabotage, theft, fire, flood |
| Infrastructure failures | Telecommunication/power failures |
| Access issues | Unauthorised physical access |
| System issues | Systems failure, human error |
| Malicious acts | Cyberattacks |
| Natural phenomena | Disasters |

**Key requirement**: Must address **physical and environmental security** of network and information systems.
# Article 4 Guidelines - Article 21(2) Requirements & Reporting

## Article 21(2) Specific Security Requirements

Sector-specific measures must include requirements covering (per Article 21(2)(a)-(j)):

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Risk analysis policy | Information system security policies |
| Incident handling | Procedures for managing incidents |
| Business continuity | Backup, disaster recovery |
| Crisis management | Response procedures |
| Supply chain security | Supplier and service provider security |
| Cryptography | Policies on encryption use |
| Access control | Human resources security |
| Asset management | Network security |
| Authentication | Multi-factor, secure communications |
| Vulnerability handling | Testing and assessment |

### Commission Implementing Acts
For digital infrastructure entities (DNS, TLD, cloud, data centres, CDN, MSP, MSSP, marketplaces, search engines, social networks, trust services):

**Deadline**: 17 October 2024 - Commission shall adopt implementing acts on technical and methodological requirements.

# Article 4 Guidelines - Reporting Significant Incidents

## II.2.1. Notification of Significant Incidents

### Who Must Be Notified (Article 23(1))
1. **CSIRT or competent authority** - without undue delay
2. **Service recipients** - if incident likely to adversely affect service provision

### What Is a "Significant" Incident?

**Definition** (Article 23(3)):
| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Has caused or capable of causing **severe operational disruption** of services OR **financial loss** for entity |
| **(b)** | Has affected or capable of affecting other persons by causing **considerable material or non-material damage** |

### Initial Assessment Factors (Recital 101)
- Affected network and information systems
- Importance in service provision
- Severity and technical characteristics of cyber threat
- Underlying vulnerabilities exploited
- Entity's experience with similar incidents

### Severity Indicators
- Extent of service functioning affected
- Duration of incident
- Number of affected service recipients

### Commission Implementing Acts
For digital infrastructure entities: Commission shall adopt implementing acts specifying when incidents are "significant" by **17 October 2024**.
# Article 4 Guidelines - Multiple-Stage Reporting Timeline

## II.2.2. Multiple-Stage Approach

### Purpose
Balance between:
- **Swift reporting** → helps mitigate spread, allows seeking assistance
- **In-depth reporting** → draws lessons, improves resilience

### NIS2 Reporting Timeline

| Stage | Deadline | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Early Warning** | **24 hours** from awareness | Alert CSIRT/authority |
| **Incident Notification** | **72 hours** from awareness | Initial assessment |
| **Intermediate Report** | Upon request | Status updates |
| **Final Report** | **1 month** after incident notification | Detailed analysis |
| **Progress Report** | At 1 month (if ongoing) | If incident still being handled |
| **Final Report (ongoing)** | **1 month** after handling complete | If incident was ongoing at 1-month mark |

### Trust Service Provider Exception
**24 hours** for incident notification (stricter than 72 hours for other entities).

---

## II.2.3. Content of Reports

### Early Warning (Article 23(4)(a))
- Indication if incident suspected of being caused by **unlawful or malicious acts**
- Indication if incident could have **cross-border impact**
- Only information necessary to make CSIRT/authority aware

### Incident Notification
- Updates to early warning information
- **Initial assessment** of severity and impact
- **Indicators of compromise** (where available)

### Intermediate Report
- Relevant status updates (if requested)

### Final Report
- Detailed description of incident (severity and impact)
- Type of threat or **root cause**
- Applied and ongoing **mitigation measures**
- **Cross-border impact** (if applicable)
# Article 4 Guidelines - Immediate Access & Consequences of Equivalence

## II.2.4. Immediate Access to Incident Notifications

### Requirement (Article 4(2)(b))
Sector-specific acts must provide CSIRTs, competent authorities, or SPOCs with **immediate access** to incident notifications.

### How to Ensure Immediate Access
- Forwarding notifications **without undue delay**
- **Automatic and direct** reporting mechanisms
- **Single entry point** complying with sector-specific act

### Equivalence Assessment Summary
Requirements in sector-specific act must:
- At minimum **correspond to** Article 23(1)-(6) requirements
- OR provide **greater detail**
- Cover: recipients, content, and time frames

# III. Consequences of Equivalence

## III.1. Supervision and Enforcement

### What Doesn't Apply When Equivalent
When sector-specific act is equivalent:
- NIS2 risk-management provisions **do not apply**
- NIS2 incident notification provisions **do not apply**
- NIS2 **Chapter VII supervision/enforcement do not apply**

### Cooperation Between Authorities
Sector-specific authorities may exercise supervisory powers **with assistance** of NIS2 competent authorities.

**Cooperation arrangements may include**:
- Coordination of supervisory activities
- Procedures for investigations and on-site inspections
- Mechanism for exchange of relevant information
- Access to cyber-related information

---

## III.2. National Cybersecurity Strategy

### Requirement (Article 7(1))
Each Member State must adopt a national cybersecurity strategy covering:
- Strategic objectives and priorities
- Sectors in Annexes I and II
- Governance framework
- Policies per Article 7(2)

**Still applies** even when sector-specific acts are equivalent for individual entities.
# Article 4 Guidelines - CSIRTs and Crisis Management

## III.3. Designation of CSIRTs

### Requirement (Article 10(1))
Member States must designate or establish CSIRTs covering:
- **All** sectors, subsectors, and entity types in Annexes I and II
- **Including** sectors with sector-specific Union legal acts

CSIRTs perform Article 11(3) tasks unless sector-specific acts specify particular tasks.

---

## III.4. National Cyber Crisis Management Frameworks and EU-CyCLONe

### National Requirements (Article 9)

Member States must:
1. Designate or establish **cyber crisis management authority(ies)**
2. Adopt **national large-scale cybersecurity incident and crisis response plan**

### Large-Scale Incident Definition (Article 6(7))
Incident that:
- Exceeds Member State's capacity to respond, OR
- Has significant impact on **at least two Member States**

### Response Plan Contents
- Cyber crisis management procedures
- Integration into general national crisis management framework
- Information exchange channels
- Relevant public and private stakeholders
- May involve **sector-specific procedures and stakeholders**

### EU-CyCLONe (Article 16)
European cyber crisis liaison organisation network:
- Supports coordinated management of large-scale incidents at operational level
- Ensures regular information exchange among Member States and EU bodies
- **Applies to all sectors** including those with sector-specific acts

### Key Point
Articles 9 and 16 **apply in their entirety** despite existence of sector-specific acts because they don't concern entity-level requirements (Articles 21, 23) or Chapter VII supervision.
# Article 4 Guidelines - Excluded NIS2 Provisions

## III.5. Exclusion of Certain NIS2 Provisions When Sector-Specific Acts Apply

### Article 3(3) and (4) - Entity Lists
**Purpose**: Establish list of essential/important entities for supervision oversight.

**When sector-specific acts apply**: Provisions should **not apply** to covered entities.
**Note**: Member States may still choose to include such entities in the list.

### Article 27(2) and (3) - Registration Information
**Purpose**: Submit information to competent authorities for ENISA registry.

**When sector-specific acts apply**: Should **not apply** to covered entities.

---

### Article 20 - Management Body Accountability

**Article 20(1)**: Management bodies must:
- **Approve** cybersecurity risk-management measures
- **Oversee** implementation
- Can be **held liable** for infringements

**Article 20(2)**: Management body members must:
- **Follow training**
- Entity encouraged to offer training to employees

**When sector-specific acts apply**: Article 20 should **not apply** because it is intrinsically linked to Article 21 requirements.

---

## Summary: What NIS2 Provisions Apply Despite Sector-Specific Acts

| NIS2 Provision | Applies? | Reason |
|----------------|----------|--------|
| Article 7 (National strategy) | **Yes** | Not about entity requirements |
| Article 9 (Crisis management) | **Yes** | Not about entity requirements |
| Article 10 (CSIRTs) | **Yes** | Must cover all sectors |
| Article 16 (EU-CyCLONe) | **Yes** | Not about entity requirements |
| Article 20 (Management liability) | **No** | Linked to Article 21 |
| Article 21 (Risk-management) | **No** | Replaced by sector-specific |
| Article 23 (Incident reporting) | **No** | Replaced by sector-specific |
| Article 27(2)-(3) (Registration) | **No** | For supervision purposes |
| Chapter VII (Supervision) | **No** | Replaced by sector-specific |
# Article 4 Guidelines - Appendix: DORA

# APPENDIX: Sector-Specific Union Legal Acts

## Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 - DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)

### Legal Basis
- **Article 1(2) DORA**: Explicitly states DORA is sector-specific act for Article 4 of NIS2
- **Recital 28 NIS2**: Confirms DORA is sector-specific for financial entities

### DORA Provisions That Replace NIS2

| DORA Article | Topic | Replaces NIS2 |
|--------------|-------|---------------|
| Article 6 et seq. | **ICT risk management** | Article 21 |
| Article 17 et seq. | **Major ICT incident reporting** | Article 23 |
| Article 24 et seq. | **Digital operational resilience testing** | - |
| Article 25 | **Information-sharing arrangements** | Article 29 |
| Article 28 et seq. | **ICT third-party risk** | - |

### Covered Financial Entities (Article 2(1)(a)-(t) DORA)
Entities in **both** DORA and NIS2 scope include:
- Credit institutions
- Trading venues
- Central counterparties (CCPs)

### What Still Applies from NIS2

| NIS2 Provision | Status |
|----------------|--------|
| National cybersecurity strategy (Article 7) | **Applies** - must include financial sector |
| CSIRTs (Article 10) | **Applies** - must cover financial sector |
| National crisis management (Article 9) | **Applies** |
| EU-CyCLONe (Article 16) | **Applies** |

### Cooperation Between DORA and NIS2 Authorities

**European Supervisory Authorities** and DORA competent authorities may:
- Request to participate in NIS2 **Cooperation Group** activities
- Exchange information with SPOCs, CSIRTs, NIS2 authorities
- Transmit major ICT incident details to NIS2 CSIRTs/authorities/SPOCs

**Methods for immediate access**:
- Direct forwarding of incident notifications
- Single entry point

---

## Key Takeaway: DORA-NIS2 Relationship

For **financial entities** covered by DORA:
- **DORA applies** for risk management and incident reporting
- **NIS2 does NOT apply** for Articles 20, 21, 23, Chapter VII
- **NIS2 STILL applies** for national strategy, CSIRTs, crisis management, EU-CyCLONe
