# NIS2 Directive - Article 1: Subject Matter

## Main Objective
Achieve a **high common level of cybersecurity across the EU** to improve the functioning of the internal market.

## Four Key Pillars

### 1. National Framework Obligations
Member States must adopt national cybersecurity strategies and designate/establish:
- Competent authorities
- Cyber crisis management authorities
- Single points of contact on cybersecurity
- CSIRTs (Computer Security Incident Response Teams)

### 2. Risk Management & Reporting
- Cybersecurity risk-management measures for entities in Annex I or II
- Reporting obligations for these entities
- Also applies to critical entities under Directive (EU) 2022/2557

### 3. Information Sharing
Rules for cybersecurity information sharing between entities

### 4. Supervision & Enforcement
Member States must supervise and enforce compliance
# NIS2 Directive - Article 2: Scope

## Who is Covered?

### Article 2(1) - Basic Coverage
**Public or private entities** that:
- Are listed in **Annex I or II**
- Qualify as **medium-sized enterprises** or larger (per Recommendation 2003/361/EC)
- Provide services or carry out activities **within the Union**

### Size Threshold (Medium Enterprise)
- 50-249 employees, OR
- Annual turnover €10-50 million, OR
- Balance sheet total €10-43 million

---

## Entities Covered REGARDLESS of Size

### Article 2(2) - Automatic Inclusion

#### (a) Critical Digital Infrastructure Providers:
- Public electronic communications networks/services
- Trust service providers
- Top-level domain (TLD) registries
- DNS service providers

#### (b) Sole Providers:
Only provider in a Member State of a service essential for critical societal/economic activities

#### (c) Public Impact:
Disruption could significantly impact public safety, security, or health

#### (d) Systemic Risk:
Disruption could cause significant systemic risk, especially cross-border

#### (e) National/Regional Importance:
Critical due to specific importance at national/regional level

#### (f) Public Administration:
- **Central government** entities
- **Regional government** entities providing critical services

---

## Article 2(3) - Critical Entities
**All entities identified as critical under Directive (EU) 2022/2557** (CER Directive) are automatically covered, regardless of size.

## Article 2(4) - Domain Registration
**Domain name registration service providers** are covered regardless of size.

## Article 2(5) - Optional Inclusions
Member States MAY include:
- Local government entities
- Education institutions (especially those doing critical research)

---

## Exclusions from NIS2

### Article 2(6) - National Security Carve-out
NIS2 does NOT affect Member States' responsibility for:
- National security
- Territorial integrity
- Maintaining law and order

### Article 2(7) - Security Sector Exclusion
**NOT covered**: Public administration entities working in:
- National security
- Public security
- Defence
- Law enforcement (including criminal investigation/prosecution)

### Article 2(8) - Partial Exemptions
Member States MAY exempt entities from **Articles 21 & 23** if they:
- Work in national security, defence, or law enforcement
- Provide services exclusively to exempted public admin entities

### Article 2(9) - Trust Services Exception
Security exemptions do **NOT apply** to trust service providers - they remain covered.

### Article 2(10) - DORA Exemption
Entities exempted from DORA (Regulation 2022/2554) under its Article 2(4) are also exempt from NIS2.

### Article 2(11) - National Security Information
NIS2 cannot require disclosure of information harming national security, public security, or defence.

---

## Relationship with Other EU Laws

### Article 2(12)
NIS2 applies **without prejudice to**:
- **GDPR** (Regulation 2016/679)
- **ePrivacy Directive** (2002/58/EC)
- **Directive 2011/93/EU** - child protection
- **Directive 2013/40/EU** - attacks against information systems
- **CER Directive** (2022/2557)

### Article 2(13-14): Data Protection
- Business confidential information may only be shared when **necessary and proportionate**
- Personal data processing must comply with **GDPR**
# NIS2 Directive - Article 3: Essential and Important Entities

## The Key Classification

NIS2 divides covered entities into two categories with different supervision levels:
- **Essential entities** = stricter supervision (proactive)
- **Important entities** = lighter supervision (reactive)

---

## Article 3(1): Who is an ESSENTIAL Entity?

### (a) Large Annex I Entities
Entities in **Annex I sectors** that are **large enterprises**:
- 250+ employees, OR
- €50M+ turnover, OR
- €43M+ balance sheet

### (b) Critical Digital Infrastructure (any size)
- Qualified trust service providers
- Top-level domain (TLD) registries
- DNS service providers

### (c) Medium Telecom Providers
Public electronic communications networks/services that are medium-sized

### (d) Central Government
Public administration entities of central government

### (e) Member State Designated
Any Annex I or II entity designated essential due to:
- Sole provider status
- Public safety/health impact
- Systemic risk potential
- National/regional importance

### (f) Critical Entities (CER Directive)
All entities identified as critical under Directive 2022/2557

### (g) Legacy OES (Optional)
Entities identified as "operators of essential services" under old NIS Directive before 16 January 2023 (if Member State chooses)

---

## Article 3(2): Who is an IMPORTANT Entity?

**Simple rule**: Any entity covered by NIS2 (in Annex I or II) that is **NOT essential** = **important entity**

---

## Article 3(3): Entity Registration

### Deadline: 17 April 2025

Member States must establish a list of:
- Essential entities
- Important entities
- Domain name registration service providers

### Ongoing Maintenance
- Review and update regularly
- At minimum every **2 years**

---

## Article 3(4): Registration Information Required

Entities must submit to competent authorities:

| Information | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **(a) Name** | Legal name of entity |
| **(b) Contact details** | Address, email, IP ranges, phone numbers |
| **(c) Sector** | Relevant sector/subsector from Annex I or II |
| **(d) Member States** | List of EU countries where services are provided |

### Update Obligation
Changes must be notified **within 2 weeks**.

### Support
- **ENISA** will provide guidelines and templates
- Member States may create self-registration mechanisms

---

## Article 3(5): Reporting to Commission

### Deadline: 17 April 2025 (then every 2 years)

Competent authorities must notify:

**(a) To Commission + Cooperation Group:**
- Number of essential/important entities per sector/subsector

**(b) To Commission only:**
- Details on entities designated under Article 2(2)(b)-(e)
- Their sectors and service types
- Which provision triggered their inclusion

## Article 3(6): Early Notification Option
Until 17 April 2025, Member States may voluntarily notify the Commission of entity names (upon request).
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 4 & 5: Lex Specialis & Minimum Harmonisation

## Article 4: Sector-Specific Union Legal Acts (Lex Specialis)

### The "DORA Principle"
**Key rule**: If another EU law already requires equivalent cybersecurity measures, NIS2 does NOT apply to those entities.

### Article 4(1): When NIS2 Steps Aside

If a sector-specific EU law requires:
- Cybersecurity risk-management measures, AND
- Incident notification

And these are **at least equivalent** to NIS2 → NIS2 provisions (including supervision) do **NOT apply**.

**Important**: If the sector law doesn't cover ALL entities in a sector, NIS2 still applies to the uncovered ones.

### Article 4(2): What is "Equivalent"?

| NIS2 Requirement | Sector Law Must Have |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Risk management (Art. 21) | At least equivalent measures |
| Incident reporting (Art. 23) | Immediate/automatic access for CSIRTs + equivalent notification rules |

### Article 4(3): Commission Guidelines
- **Deadline**: 17 July 2023 (guidelines issued)
- Regular review
- Input from Cooperation Group and ENISA

**Practical example**: DORA (Regulation 2022/2554) for financial sector entities

---

## Article 5: Minimum Harmonisation

Member States **MAY adopt stricter rules** than NIS2, provided they:
- Ensure higher cybersecurity
- Are consistent with EU law

**This means**: NIS2 is a floor, not a ceiling.
# NIS2 Directive - Article 6: Definitions

All 41 definitions used in the NIS2 Directive.

---

## Core System Definitions (1-4)

### (1) Network and Information System
Three components:
- **(a)** Electronic communications networks (per Directive 2018/1972)
- **(b)** Any device/group of devices that automatically process digital data via a programme
- **(c)** Digital data stored/processed/transmitted by the above

### (2) Security of Network and Information Systems
The ability to **resist events** that could compromise:
- **A**vailability
- **A**uthenticity
- **I**ntegrity
- **C**onfidentiality

Of data or services offered by those systems.

### (3) Cybersecurity
Defined by reference to Regulation (EU) 2019/881 (Cybersecurity Act).

### (4) National Cybersecurity Strategy
A coherent framework providing:
- Strategic objectives
- Priorities
- Governance mechanisms

---

## Incident Definitions (5-8)

### (5) Near Miss
An event that **could have** compromised availability, authenticity, integrity, or confidentiality but:
- Was successfully prevented, OR
- Did not materialise

### (6) Incident
An event that **actually compromises** availability, authenticity, integrity, or confidentiality of data or services.

### (7) Large-Scale Cybersecurity Incident
An incident that:
- Exceeds a single Member State's response capacity, OR
- Significantly impacts **2+ Member States**

### (8) Incident Handling
Actions and procedures for:
- **Prevent** - stop incidents before they occur
- **Detect** - identify when incidents happen
- **Analyse** - understand what happened
- **Contain** - limit the damage
- **Respond** - take action
- **Recover** - restore normal operations

---

## Risk & Threat Definitions (9-11)

### (9) Risk
**Formula**: Risk = Magnitude of loss × Likelihood of occurrence

### (10) Cyber Threat
Defined by Regulation (EU) 2019/881 (Cybersecurity Act).

### (11) Significant Cyber Threat
A cyber threat with potential to:
- Severely impact an entity's network/information systems
- Cause **considerable material or non-material damage** to users

---

## ICT Definitions (12-15)

### (12) ICT Product
Hardware/software products (see Reg. 2019/881)

### (13) ICT Service
Services delivered via ICT (see Reg. 2019/881)

### (14) ICT Process
Processes using ICT (see Reg. 2019/881)

### (15) Vulnerability
A **weakness, susceptibility, or flaw** in ICT products/services that can be exploited by a cyber threat.

---

## Standards Definitions (16-17)

### (16) Standard
Technical standard per EU standardization regulation (Reg. 1025/2012)

### (17) Technical Specification
Detailed technical requirements per EU standardization regulation

---

## Internet Infrastructure Definitions (18-22)

### (18) Internet Exchange Point (IXP)
A network facility that:
- Interconnects **2+ independent networks** (autonomous systems)
- Facilitates internet traffic exchange
- Only provides interconnection for autonomous systems
- Does NOT require traffic to pass through third parties
- Does NOT alter or interfere with traffic

### (19) Domain Name System (DNS)
A hierarchical distributed naming system that:
- Identifies internet services and resources
- Allows devices to use routing/connectivity services

### (20) DNS Service Provider
An entity providing:
- **(a)** Public recursive DNS resolution for end-users, OR
- **(b)** Authoritative DNS resolution for third parties

**Exception**: Root name servers are excluded.

### (21) Top-Level Domain (TLD) Name Registry
An entity that:
- Has been delegated a specific TLD (e.g., .com, .se, .eu)
- Administers the TLD including registration, technical operation, database maintenance

### (22) Entity Providing Domain Name Registration Services
Includes:
- **Registrars** (directly register domains)
- **Agents** acting on behalf of registrars
- Privacy/proxy registration providers
- Resellers

---

## Digital Service Definitions (23)

### (23) Digital Service
Defined by Directive (EU) 2015/1535 - Information Society services delivered electronically.

---

## Trust Services (24-27)

### (24) Trust Service
Electronic services for creating, verifying, validating electronic signatures, seals, timestamps, etc.

### (25) Trust Service Provider
Entity providing trust services

### (26) Qualified Trust Service
Trust service meeting eIDAS requirements for qualified status

### (27) Qualified Trust Service Provider
Provider meeting requirements for qualified status under eIDAS (Reg. 910/2014)

---

## Online Platform Definitions (28-29)

### (28) Online Marketplace
Platform allowing consumers to conclude contracts with traders online (per Directive 2005/29/EC)

### (29) Online Search Engine
Digital service allowing users to search websites based on queries (per Regulation 2019/1150)

---

## Cloud & Digital Infrastructure (30-33)

### (30) Cloud Computing Service
A digital service enabling:
- **On-demand administration**
- Broad remote access
- Scalable and elastic pool of **shareable computing resources**
- May be distributed across multiple locations

### (31) Data Centre Service
A service encompassing:
- Structures dedicated to centralised accommodation of IT/network equipment
- Data storage, processing, and transport services
- Power distribution facilities
- Environmental control infrastructure

### (32) Content Delivery Network (CDN)
A network of **geographically distributed servers** providing:
- High availability
- Accessibility
- Fast delivery of digital content/services

### (33) Social Networking Services Platform
A platform enabling users to:
- Connect, Share, Discover, Communicate
- Across multiple devices via chats, posts, videos, recommendations

---

## Entity & Administration Definitions (34-41)

### (34) Representative
A person/entity **established in the EU** designated to act on behalf of **non-EU entities** for NIS2 compliance purposes.

### (35) Public Administration Entity
An entity recognised by national law meeting ALL criteria:

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **(a) Purpose** | Meeting general interest needs; NOT industrial/commercial |
| **(b) Legal status** | Has legal personality |
| **(c) Control** | Mainly state-financed OR state-supervised OR >50% board appointed by state |
| **(d) Powers** | Can make administrative/regulatory decisions affecting cross-border movement |

**Exclusions**: Judiciary, parliaments, central banks

### (36) Public Electronic Communications Network
Per Directive (EU) 2018/1972 (European Electronic Communications Code)

### (37) Electronic Communications Service
Per Directive (EU) 2018/1972

### (38) Entity
A natural or legal person that:
- Is created and recognised under national law
- Can exercise rights and be subject to obligations in its own name

### (39) Managed Service Provider (MSP)
An entity providing services for installation, management, operation, maintenance of ICT products, networks, infrastructure, applications, or information systems.

### (40) Managed Security Service Provider (MSSP)
An MSP specifically providing **cybersecurity risk management** services.

### (41) Research Organisation
Entity with primary goal of applied research or experimental development for commercial exploitation.
**Excludes**: Educational institutions.
# NIS2 Directive - Article 7: National Cybersecurity Strategy

## Obligation
Each Member State **must adopt** a national cybersecurity strategy.

---

## Article 7(1): Required Content

### (a) Objectives & Priorities
- Cybersecurity strategy objectives
- Cover sectors in Annexes I and II

### (b) Governance Framework
- How to achieve objectives
- Include policies from paragraph 2

### (c) Stakeholder Framework
Clarify roles and responsibilities enabling cooperation between:
- Competent authorities
- Single points of contact
- CSIRTs
- Sector-specific authorities

### (d) Asset & Risk Identification
- Mechanism to identify relevant assets
- Risk assessment for the Member State

### (e) Incident Response
Measures for preparedness, responsiveness, and recovery (including public-private cooperation)

### (f) Stakeholder List
All authorities and stakeholders involved in strategy implementation

### (g) CER Directive Coordination
Policy framework for coordination between NIS2 and CER Directive authorities for sharing information on risks, threats, incidents

### (h) Citizen Awareness
Plan with measures to enhance public cybersecurity awareness

---

## Article 7(2): Required Policies

| Policy Area | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **(a) Supply Chain Security** | Cybersecurity for ICT products/services in entity supply chains |
| **(b) Public Procurement** | Cybersecurity requirements for ICT in public procurement |
| **(c) Vulnerability Management** | Promote coordinated vulnerability disclosure |
| **(d) Internet Core Protection** | Sustain availability, integrity, confidentiality of public internet core |
| **(e) Advanced Technology** | Promote state-of-the-art cybersecurity technologies |
| **(f) Education & Awareness** | Cybersecurity education, training, skills, awareness, cyber hygiene |
| **(g) Academic Support** | Support research institutions to develop cybersecurity tools |
| **(h) Information Sharing Tools** | Procedures for voluntary cybersecurity information sharing |
| **(i) SME Support** | Strengthen cyber resilience for SMEs (guidance, assistance) |
| **(j) Active Cyber Protection** | Promote proactive defensive measures |

---

## Article 7(3-4): Strategy Administration

### Notification
- Submit strategy to Commission within **3 months** of adoption
- May exclude national security information

### Review Cycle
- Assess using **key performance indicators (KPIs)**
- Review at least every **5 years**
- Update as necessary
- **ENISA** provides assistance upon request
# NIS2 Directive - Article 8: Competent Authorities & Single Points of Contact

## Competent Authorities

### Article 8(1): Designation
Each Member State must designate/establish **one or more competent authorities** for:
- Cybersecurity
- Supervisory tasks (Chapter VII)

### Article 8(2): Role
Monitor NIS2 implementation at national level.

---

## Single Point of Contact

### Article 8(3): Requirement
Each Member State must designate/establish **one single point of contact**.

If only one competent authority exists → it is also the single point of contact.

### Article 8(4): Functions
Liaison for:
- **Cross-border cooperation** with other Member States
- Coordination with Commission and ENISA
- **Cross-sectoral cooperation** within the Member State

---

## Resources & Notification

### Article 8(5): Adequate Resources
Competent authorities and single points of contact must have **adequate resources** for effective task execution.

### Article 8(6): Transparency

| Action | Responsible |
|--------|-------------|
| Notify Commission of competent authority & single point of contact | Member State |
| Make competent authority identity public | Member State |
| Publish list of single points of contact | Commission |

Notify without undue delay + any subsequent changes.
# NIS2 Directive - Article 9: National Cyber Crisis Management Frameworks

## Cyber Crisis Management Authority

### Article 9(1): Designation
Each Member State must designate/establish **one or more authorities** for:
- Managing large-scale cybersecurity incidents
- Managing cyber crises

**Requirements**:
- Adequate resources
- Coherence with existing national crisis management frameworks

### Article 9(2): Coordinator
If multiple authorities → must clearly designate **one coordinator** for large-scale incidents/crises.

### Article 9(3): Resource Identification
Each Member State must identify capabilities, assets, and procedures that can be deployed in a crisis.

---

## National Crisis Response Plan

### Article 9(4): Required Plan

Each Member State must adopt a **national large-scale cybersecurity incident and crisis response plan**.

### Plan Must Include:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **(a) Preparedness objectives** | Goals for national preparedness measures and activities |
| **(b) Authority responsibilities** | Tasks of cyber crisis management authorities |
| **(c) Procedures** | Crisis management procedures, integration with national framework, information exchange channels |
| **(d) Preparedness measures** | Including exercises and training |
| **(e) Stakeholders** | Relevant public and private stakeholders and infrastructure |
| **(f) EU-level coordination** | Procedures for effective participation in EU-level coordinated management |

---

## Notification Requirements

### Article 9(5): Deadlines

| What | To Whom | When |
|------|---------|------|
| Identity of cyber crisis authority | Commission | Within **3 months** of designation |
| Crisis response plan information | Commission + **EU-CyCLONe** | Within **3 months** of plan adoption |

**Note**: May exclude national security information.
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 10 & 11: CSIRTs

## Article 10: CSIRT Establishment

### Article 10(1): Designation
- Each Member State must designate/establish **one or more CSIRTs**
- May be within a competent authority
- Must cover **all sectors in Annexes I and II**
- Responsible for incident handling

### Article 10(2-3): Resources & Infrastructure
- **Adequate resources** for Article 11(3) tasks
- Secure, resilient communication infrastructure
- Contribute to **secure information-sharing tools**

### Article 10(4-6): Cooperation
- Cooperate with sectoral/cross-sectoral communities
- Participate in **peer reviews** (Article 19)
- Ensure effective cooperation in **CSIRTs network**

### Article 10(7-8): International Cooperation
- May cooperate with **non-EU CSIRTs** using protocols like Traffic Light Protocol
- May provide cybersecurity assistance to third countries

### Article 10(9): Notification
Notify Commission of CSIRT identity without undue delay.

---

## Article 11: CSIRT Requirements & Tasks

### Article 11(1): Mandatory Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **(a) Communication** | High availability, no single points of failure, multiple contact methods |
| **(b) Physical security** | Premises and systems at secure sites |
| **(c) Request management** | Effective system for managing and routing requests |
| **(d) Operational security** | Confidentiality and trustworthiness |
| **(e) Staffing** | Adequate staffing for **24/7 availability**, trained staff |
| **(f) Business continuity** | Redundant systems, backup working space |

---

### Article 11(3): CSIRT Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **(a) Monitoring** | Monitor cyber threats, vulnerabilities, incidents at national level |
| **(b) Early Warning** | Provide warnings, alerts, announcements to entities (near real-time) |
| **(c) Incident Response** | Respond to incidents and assist entities |
| **(d) Forensics** | Collect/analyse forensic data, provide situational awareness |
| **(e) Vulnerability Scanning** | Proactive scanning upon request |
| **(f) Network Participation** | Participate in CSIRTs network, provide mutual assistance |
| **(g) Vulnerability Disclosure** | Act as coordinator (Article 12) |
| **(h) Information Sharing** | Contribute to secure information-sharing tools |

### Proactive Scanning Powers
CSIRTs **may** conduct **proactive non-intrusive scanning** of publicly accessible systems to detect vulnerabilities.
- Must NOT negatively impact entity services
- May use risk-based prioritization

### Article 11(4-5): Cooperation
- Establish cooperation with **private sector stakeholders**
- Promote common/standardized incident-handling practices
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 12 & 13: Vulnerability Disclosure & Cooperation

## Article 12: Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure

### Article 12(1): Coordinator Role
Each Member State must designate **one CSIRT as coordinator** for coordinated vulnerability disclosure.

**Role**: Trusted intermediary between:
- Person reporting vulnerability
- Manufacturer/provider of vulnerable ICT products/services

### Coordinator Tasks
| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Identify and contact affected entities |
| **(b)** | Assist persons reporting vulnerabilities |
| **(c)** | Negotiate disclosure timelines, manage multi-entity vulnerabilities |

### Anonymous Reporting
- **Anonymous reporting** option must be available
- Anonymity protection for reporters
- Cross-border: Coordinators must cooperate within CSIRTs network

---

## Article 12(2): European Vulnerability Database

### ENISA Responsibility
Develop and maintain:
- **European vulnerability database**
- Supporting systems, policies, procedures
- Security and integrity measures

### Database Access
- **All entities** (within or outside NIS2 scope)
- Suppliers of network and information systems
- **Voluntary** disclosure and registration
- **All stakeholders** have access

### Database Must Include:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Vulnerability description |
| **(b)** | Affected ICT products/services and severity |
| **(c)** | Remediation (patches or mitigation guidance) |

---

## Article 13: Cooperation at National Level

### Article 13(1): Internal Cooperation
Where separate, the following **must cooperate**:
- Competent authorities
- Single point of contact
- CSIRTs

### Article 13(2): Specific Cooperation Areas
- Incident handling and supervision
- Information exchange on incidents, cyber threats, risks, vulnerabilities
- Cross-border incident coordination
- Training assistance
- Mutual cooperation mechanisms

### Article 13(3): Security Entity Coordination
NIS2 authorities must cooperate with security-related authorities (e.g., under GDPR, CER Directive).

| **(l)** | Develop contribution for Commission reports |

### Article 15(4): Reports
Every **two years**: Report to Cooperation Group on operational cooperation progress, conclusions, recommendations.
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 16, 17, 18: EU-CyCLONe & International Cooperation

## Article 16: EU-CyCLONe (European Cyber Crisis Liaison Organisation Network)

### Article 16(1): Purpose
- Support **coordinated management** of large-scale cybersecurity incidents/crises at **operational level**
- Ensure regular information exchange among Member States and EU institutions

### Article 16(2): Composition

| Role | Participants |
|------|--------------|
| **Members** | Member States' cyber crisis management authorities |
| **Commission** | Full participant (if large-scale incident impacts NIS2 services); otherwise observer |
| **Secretariat** | ENISA |
| **Observers** | May invite relevant stakeholders |

### Article 16(3): Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Increase **preparedness** for managing large-scale incidents |
| **(b)** | Develop **shared situational awareness** |
| **(c)** | Assess consequences/impact, propose **mitigation measures** |
| **(d)** | Coordinate management, support **political-level decision-making** |
| **(e)** | Discuss national crisis response plans upon request |

### Article 16(4-7): Governance
- Adopt own rules of procedure
- Regular reports to **Cooperation Group** on large-scale incident management
- Coordinate with CSIRTs network on incident response

---

## Article 17: International Cooperation

### Article 17(1): Union's Objectives
The Union may enter into **international agreements** with third countries or international organisations allowing participation in:
- Cooperation Group activities (certain tasks)
- CSIRTs network activities (certain tasks)
- EU-CyCLONe (certain circumstances)

### Article 17(2): Agreement Requirements
Agreements must ensure **adequate level of protection** for personal data.

---

## Article 18: Report on Essential & Important Entities

### Article 18(1): ENISA Report
**Deadline**: 17 April 2025, then every **two years**

ENISA shall submit to Parliament and Council a report on:
- Number of essential/important entities per sector
- Number of entities registered per Article 27
- Information submitted per Article 3(5)

### Purpose
Assess implementation and monitor number of entities in scope.
# NIS2 Directive - Article 19: Peer Reviews

## Article 19(1): Framework

### Deadline
**17 January 2025**: Cooperation Group (with Commission, ENISA, CSIRTs network) establishes methodology.

### Purpose
- Learn from shared experiences
- Strengthen mutual trust
- Achieve high common cybersecurity level
- Enhance Member State capabilities and policies

### Key Rules
- **Voluntary** participation
- Conducted by **cybersecurity experts**
- Experts designated by **at least 2 Member States** (not the one being reviewed)

### Scope - At Least One of:

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Implementation of Articles 21 and 23 (risk management & reporting) |
| **(b)** | Authority capabilities (financial, technical, human resources) |
| **(c)** | CSIRT operational capabilities |
| **(d)** | Mutual assistance implementation (Article 37) |
| **(e)** | Information sharing arrangements (Article 29) |
| **(f)** | Specific cross-border/cross-sector issues |

---

## Article 19(2-5): Process

### Expert Selection
Methodology must include **objective, non-discriminatory, fair, transparent criteria**.

**Observers**: Commission and ENISA participate as observers.

### Pre-Review
- Member States may identify specific issues for review
- Notify participants of scope before commencing
- May provide self-assessment to experts

---

## Article 19(6-9): Reports & Follow-up

### Article 19(6): Peer Review Report
Experts draft report including:
- Findings
- Conclusions
- Recommendations

### Article 19(7): Translation
Report transmitted to reviewed Member State in English; translated to local language upon request.

### Article 19(8): Cooperation Group Discussion
- Discussed (without presence of reviewed Member State)
- Draw conclusions and recommendations

### Article 19(9): Publication
- Report submitted to Commission and Cooperation Group
- Member State **may authorise public release** (full or summary)

---

## Article 19(10): Implementing Acts

**Deadline**: 17 October 2024

Commission may adopt implementing acts specifying:
- Areas for peer reviews
- Detailed methodology and process
- Content of self-assessments
- Expert selection criteria
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 20, 21, 22: Governance & Risk Management

## Article 20: Governance

### Article 20(1): Management Body Responsibility
Management bodies of essential and important entities must:
- **Approve** cybersecurity risk-management measures (Article 21)
- **Oversee** implementation
- Can be **held liable** for infringements

### Article 20(2): Training Requirements

**Management Body Training** - Members **must receive training** to:
- Identify risks
- Assess cybersecurity risk-management practices
- Understand impact on entity services

**Employee Training** - Entities **should offer similar training** to employees regularly

---

## Article 21: Cybersecurity Risk-Management Measures

### THE CORE OBLIGATION

## Article 21(1): Requirement

Essential and important entities must take **appropriate and proportionate** measures:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Technical** | Technology-based controls |
| **Operational** | Process and procedure controls |
| **Organisational** | Governance and management controls |

### Goals
1. Manage risks to network and information systems
2. **Prevent or minimise** incident impact on service recipients and other services

### Proportionality Factors
Measures must consider:
- **State-of-the-art** technology
- Relevant European/international **standards**
- **Cost** of implementation
- Entity's **risk exposure** and **size**
- **Likelihood** and **severity** of incidents

---

## Article 21(2): THE 10 MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

Based on **all-hazards approach** protecting systems AND physical environment.

| # | Measure | Description |
|---|---------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Risk analysis & security policies | Policies on risk analysis and information system security |
| **(b)** | Incident handling | Procedures for incident detection, response, recovery |
| **(c)** | Business continuity | Backup management, disaster recovery, crisis management |
| **(d)** | Supply chain security | Security of supplier/service provider relationships |
| **(e)** | Secure development | Security in acquisition, development, maintenance; vulnerability handling |
| **(f)** | Effectiveness assessment | Policies to assess if measures work |
| **(g)** | Cyber hygiene & training | Basic practices and cybersecurity training |
| **(h)** | Cryptography | Policies on cryptography and encryption use |
| **(i)** | HR & access control | Human resources security, access control, asset management |
| **(j)** | MFA & secure communications | Multi-factor authentication; secured voice/video/text; emergency communications |

---

## Article 21(3): Supply Chain Security Requirements

When assessing supply chain measures, entities must consider:
- **Vulnerabilities** specific to each direct supplier/service provider
- **Overall quality** of supplier products
- Supplier **cybersecurity practices** including secure development
- Results of **coordinated EU supply chain risk assessments** (Article 22)

## Article 21(4): Non-Compliance Correction
If non-compliance found → Must take **corrective measures without undue delay**

## Article 21(5): Implementing Acts

**Mandatory by 17 October 2024** for:
- DNS service providers, TLD registries
- Cloud computing, Data centres, CDN providers
- MSPs, MSSPs
- Online marketplaces, Search engines, Social networks
- Trust service providers

---

## Article 22: EU-Level Coordinated Supply Chain Risk Assessments

### Article 22(1): Authority
**Cooperation Group** (with Commission and ENISA) **may** carry out coordinated risk assessments of critical ICT supply chains.

### Article 22(2): Identification
Commission identifies critical ICT services/systems/products for coordinated assessment.
# NIS2 Directive - Article 23: Reporting Obligations

## THE INCIDENT NOTIFICATION REGIME

## Article 23(1): Notification Requirement

Essential and important entities must notify **CSIRT or competent authority** of any **significant incident**.

### Key Points:
- Notification must be **without undue delay**
- Must include information for cross-border impact assessment
- **Notification does NOT increase liability** of the entity
- Cross-border/cross-sectoral incidents → single points of contact informed

### Service Recipient Notification
Entities **should notify service recipients** of significant incidents likely to affect service provision.

---

## Article 23(2): Cyber Threat Communication

For **significant cyber threats**, entities must communicate to potentially affected service recipients:
- Measures/remedies recipients can take
- The threat itself (where appropriate)

---

## Article 23(3): What is a "Significant Incident"?

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **(a) Entity impact** | Has caused OR is capable of causing **severe operational disruption** or **financial loss** to the entity |
| **(b) Third-party impact** | Has affected OR is capable of affecting others by causing **considerable material or non-material damage** |

---

## Article 23(4): THE NOTIFICATION TIMELINE

| Stage | Deadline | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **(a) Early Warning** | **24 hours** | Suspected malicious acts? Cross-border impact? |
| **(b) Incident Notification** | **72 hours** | Update early warning + initial assessment (severity, impact, IoCs) |
| **(c) Intermediate Report** | Upon request | Status updates as requested by CSIRT/authority |
| **(d) Final Report** | **1 month** after (b) | See below |
| **(e) Ongoing Incident** | Progress report at (d) deadline | Final report within 1 month of handling completion |

### Final Report Must Include:
1. Detailed description (severity and impact)
2. Type of threat or **root cause**
3. Applied and ongoing **mitigation measures**
4. Cross-border impact (if applicable)

### Trust Service Provider Exception
**24 hours** for BOTH early warning AND incident notification (stricter than standard 72 hours).

---

## Article 23(5): CSIRT/Authority Response

### Timeline
Respond **within 24 hours** of early warning (where possible).

### Response Must Include:
- Initial feedback on incident
- Guidance/operational advice on mitigation (upon request)
- Additional technical support (upon request)
- If suspected **criminal**: guidance on reporting to law enforcement

---

## Article 23(6): Cross-Border Notification

If incident affects **2+ Member States**:

CSIRT/authority/single point of contact must inform:
- Other affected Member States
- ENISA

**Protection**: Must preserve entity's security, commercial interests, and confidentiality.

---

## Article 23(7): Public Disclosure

CSIRT or competent authority **may** inform the public when:
- Public awareness needed to **prevent** or **deal with** incident
- Disclosure is in **public interest**

**Process**: Must consult entity concerned first.

---

## Article 23(9): ENISA Reporting

### Quarterly Reports
Single point of contact submits to ENISA every **3 months**:
- **Anonymised and aggregated** data on incidents, threats, near misses

### ENISA Actions
- Informs Cooperation Group and CSIRTs network of findings every **6 months**

---

## Article 23(11): Implementing Acts

**Mandatory by 17 October 2024** for digital infrastructure providers:
- Specify **when incident is "significant"**
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 24 & 25: Certification & Standardisation

## Article 24: European Cybersecurity Certification Schemes

### Article 24(1): Member State Options
To demonstrate Article 21 compliance, Member States **may require** entities to use:
- ICT products, services, processes **certified under EU cybersecurity certification schemes** (Regulation 2019/881)
- Applies to developed or procured products

**Encouragement**: Member States should encourage use of **qualified trust services**.

### Article 24(2): Commission Delegated Acts
Commission may adopt delegated acts specifying:
- Which entity categories must use certified products/services
- Which must obtain certification

**Conditions**:
- Only when insufficient cybersecurity levels identified
- Must include implementation period
- Requires impact assessment and consultations

### Article 24(3): Missing Schemes
If no appropriate certification scheme exists:
- Commission may request ENISA to prepare candidate scheme
- After consulting Cooperation Group and European Cybersecurity Certification Group

---

## Article 25: Standardisation

### Article 25(1): Technology Neutrality
Member States must:
- Encourage use of **European and international standards**
- **NOT impose or favour** particular technology types

### Article 25(2): ENISA Guidance
ENISA (with Member States and stakeholders) shall provide:
- Advice on technical areas for standards
- Guidelines on existing applicable standards (including national)
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 26, 27, 28: Jurisdiction, Registration & WHOIS

## Article 26: Jurisdiction and Territoriality

### Article 26(1): General Rule
Entities fall under jurisdiction of **Member State where established**.

### Exceptions

| Entity Type | Jurisdiction |
|-------------|--------------|
| **(a) Telecom** | Where they **provide services** |
| **(b) Digital Infrastructure** | Where **main establishment** is |
| **(c) Public Administration** | Where **established them** |

### Article 26(2): Determining Main Establishment

| Priority | Criterion |
|----------|-----------|
| **1st** | Where **cybersecurity risk-management decisions** are predominantly taken |
| **2nd** | Where **cybersecurity operations** are carried out |
| **3rd** | Where establishment has **highest number of employees** in EU |

### Article 26(3): Non-EU Entities
If digital infrastructure entity **not established in EU**:
- Must designate **EU representative**
- Falls under jurisdiction where representative is established
- **Without representative**: any Member State may take legal action

---

## Article 27: Registry of Entities

### Article 27(1): ENISA Registry
ENISA must create and maintain a registry of digital infrastructure entities:
- DNS, TLD, domain registration services
- Cloud, data centres, CDN
- MSP, MSSP
- Online marketplaces, search engines, social networks

### Article 27(2): Registration Information

**Deadline: 17 January 2025**

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Entity name |
| **(b)** | Sector, subsector, entity type |
| **(c)** | Main establishment address + other EU establishments |
| **(d)** | Contact details (email, phone) |
| **(e)** | Member States where services provided |
| **(f)** | IP ranges |

### Article 27(3): Update Obligation
Changes must be notified **within 3 months**.

---

## Article 28: Database of Domain Name Registration Data (WHOIS)

### Article 28(1): Collection Obligation
TLD registries and domain registration services must:
- Collect and maintain **accurate and complete** domain registration data
- Use dedicated database with **due diligence**
- Comply with **GDPR** for personal data

### Purpose
Contribute to DNS **security, stability, and resilience**.

### Article 28(2): Required Data
Database must enable identification and contact of:
- Domain name **holders**
- **Points of contact** administering domains

**Required fields**: Domain name, registration date, registrant name/email/phone/address

### Article 28(3): Policies
Maintain policies and procedures for accurate data including verification procedures.

### Article 28(4): Public Access
Make non-personal registration data **publicly available** without undue delay after registration.

### Article 28(5): Legitimate Access Requests
Respond to **lawful and duly substantiated requests** within **72 hours** from:
- Competent authorities under Union or national law
- Entities seeking access for cybersecurity purposes

### Article 28(6): Data Retention
Retain data for **reasonable period** after domain name lapse (per GDPR).
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 29, 30, 31: Information Sharing & General Supervision

## Article 29: Cybersecurity Information-Sharing Arrangements

### Article 29(1): Voluntary Sharing
Essential and important entities **may** exchange relevant cybersecurity information:
- Cyber threats
- Vulnerabilities
- Indicators of compromise
- Tactics, techniques, and procedures
- Alerts
- Configuration tools

### Purpose
- **Prevent** incidents
- **Detect** incidents
- **Respond** to incidents
- **Recover** from incidents
- **Mitigate** impact

### Article 29(2): Sharing Communities
Exchange within communities of:
- Essential and important entities
- Their suppliers/service providers (where relevant)

Must use **cybersecurity information-sharing arrangements** respecting sensitive nature.

### Article 29(3): Member State Facilitation
Member States shall facilitate establishment of arrangements specifying:
- Operational elements
- Dedicated ICT platforms
- Automation tools
- Content and conditions

### Article 29(4): Notification
Entities must **notify competent authorities** when **entering** or **withdrawing** from arrangements.

### Article 29(5): ENISA Support
ENISA provides best practices exchange and guidance.

---

## Article 30: Voluntary Notification

### Article 30(1): Who Can Submit

| Entity Type | What They Can Report |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Essential/important entities | Incidents, cyber threats, near misses |
| **Any other entity** (even outside NIS2 scope) | **Significant** incidents, cyber threats, near misses |

### Article 30(2): Processing
**Process**: Same as mandatory notifications (Article 23 procedure)

**Priority**: May prioritize **mandatory over voluntary** notifications.

**Key Protection**: Voluntary reporting shall **NOT** result in additional obligations.

---

## Article 31: General Aspects of Supervision & Enforcement

### Article 31(1): Effectiveness
Member States must ensure competent authorities **effectively** monitor and take measures for NIS2 compliance.

### Article 31(2): Proportionality
Competent authorities must exercise powers **objectively and proportionately**.

### Article 31(3): Sectoral Expertise
May request assistance from authorities responsible for sectoral acts (e.g., DORA) for technical expertise.

### Article 31(4): Cross-Border Cooperation
Competent authorities must **closely cooperate** with data protection authorities when addressing incidents involving personal data breaches.
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 32-35: Supervision & Enforcement

## Article 32: Supervision of Essential Entities

### Article 32(1): Measure Requirements
Measures must be: **Effective, Proportionate, Dissuasive**

### Article 32(2): Supervisory Powers

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **(a)** On-site inspections | Including off-site supervision and **random checks** |
| **(b)** Regular security audits | Targeted audits by independent body or authority |
| **(c)** Ad hoc audits | Justified by significant incident or NIS2 infringement |
| **(d)** Security scans | Based on objective, non-discriminatory criteria |
| **(e)** Information requests | To assess risk-management measures |
| **(f)** Data access requests | Access to documents for supervision |
| **(g)** Implementation evidence | Results of security audits |

**Cost**: Paid by audited entity (unless authority decides otherwise)

### Article 32(4): Enforcement Powers

| Power | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **(a)** Warnings | Issue warnings about infringements |
| **(b)** Binding instructions | Orders with deadlines to prevent/remedy incidents |
| **(c)** Cease orders | Order to stop infringing conduct |
| **(d)** Compliance orders | Order compliance with Article 21 or 23 |
| **(e)** User notification | Order to inform users of cyber threats |
| **(f)** Audit implementation | Order to implement audit recommendations |
| **(g)** Monitoring officer | Designate officer to oversee compliance |
| **(h)** Public disclosure | Order to publicise infringements |
| **(i)** Administrative fines | Per Article 34, **in addition** to (a)-(h) |

### Article 32(5): Escalation Powers (When Measures Fail)

If measures (a)-(d) and (f) are **ineffective** and deadline not met:

| Escalation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **(a)** Suspend certification | Temporarily suspend for part or all services |
| **(b)** Management ban | Temporarily prohibit **CEO or legal representative** from managerial functions |

**Limitations**:
- Apply only **until entity remedies** deficiencies
- Subject to **procedural safeguards**
- **NOT applicable** to public administration entities

---

## Article 33: Supervision of Important Entities

### Key Difference: Ex Post Supervision
Competent authorities can take action **after** evidence of non-compliance (not proactive like essential entities).

### Article 33(4): Enforcement Powers

Same as essential entities EXCEPT:
- **No monitoring officer** (32(4)(g))
- **No certification suspension or management ban** (32(5))

---

## Article 34: Administrative Fines

### Article 34(1): General Principle
Fines must be **effective, proportionate, and dissuasive**.

### Article 34(4): Maximum Fines for ESSENTIAL Entities

| Calculation | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Fixed maximum | **€10,000,000** |
| OR percentage | **2%** of total worldwide annual turnover |
| **Whichever is higher** | |

### Article 34(5): Maximum Fines for IMPORTANT Entities

| Calculation | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Fixed maximum | **€7,000,000** |
| OR percentage | **1.4%** of total worldwide annual turnover |
| **Whichever is higher** | |

### Article 34(6): Periodic Penalty Payments
Member States **may** provide for periodic payments to compel compliance.

### Article 34(7): Public Administration
Member States decide **whether and to what extent** fines apply to public administration entities.

---

## Article 35: GDPR Coordination

### Article 35(1): Notification to GDPR Authorities
If NIS2 infringement **can entail a personal data breach**:
- Must **inform GDPR supervisory authorities** without undue delay

### Article 35(2): No Double Fines
**Key rule**: If GDPR authority imposes fine for same conduct:
- NIS2 authority shall **NOT** impose additional fine
- BUT **may still** impose other enforcement measures

---

## Summary: Essential vs Important Supervision

| Aspect | Essential Entities | Important Entities |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Supervision type** | Proactive (ex ante) | Reactive (ex post) |
| **Random checks** | Yes | Only with evidence |
| **Monitoring officer** | Yes | No |
| **Certification suspension** | Yes | No |
| **Management ban** | Yes | No |
| **Maximum fine** | €10M / 2% turnover | €7M / 1.4% turnover |
# NIS2 Directive - Articles 36-39: Penalties, Mutual Assistance & Delegated Acts

## Article 36: Penalties

### Article 36(1): Requirement
Member States must lay down rules on **penalties** for infringements of national NIS2 measures.

### Article 36(2): Characteristics
Penalties must be:
- **Effective**
- **Proportionate**
- **Dissuasive**

### Article 36(3): Notification
**Deadline**: 17 October 2024 - notify Commission of rules and subsequent amendments.

---

## Article 37: Mutual Assistance

### Article 37(1): Principle
If entity provides services in **multiple Member States**, authorities must cooperate and assist each other.

### Article 37(2): Assistance Scope
Assistance includes:
- Supervisory measures
- Enforcement measures
- Information exchange

### Article 37(3): Joint Supervision
Authorities may **jointly supervise** entities operating in multiple Member States.

### Article 37(4): Consultation
Before taking measures affecting entity in another Member State → **consult** that Member State's authority.

### Article 37(5): Request Response
Respond to assistance requests **without undue delay**, including:
- Reason for request
- Available information
- Relevant investigative measures

### Article 37(6): Refusal
May refuse request only if:
- Insufficient competence
- Disproportionate burden
- National security reasons
- Must provide reasons

---

## Article 38: Delegated Acts

### Article 38(1): Delegation
Commission is empowered to adopt **delegated acts** for:
- Article 24(2) - certification requirements
- Article 37 - mutual assistance procedures

### Article 38(2): Duration
Power conferred for **five years** from 16 January 2023.

### Article 38(3): Revocation
Parliament or Council may **revoke** delegation at any time.

### Article 38(4-6): Objection Period
- Delegated act enters into force if no objection within **two months**
- May be extended by two months at Parliament/Council initiative

---

## Article 39: Committee Procedure

### Article 39(1): Committee
Commission is assisted by a **committee** (Regulation (EU) No 182/2011).

### Article 39(2): Examination Procedure
For implementing acts under Articles 21(5), 23(11), 27(10), 32(9), 33(7), 37(7).

---
